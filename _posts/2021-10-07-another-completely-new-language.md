English can stand as a difficult language for someone who learned and used a language which is far from different to English. But for those who do not even have latin letters in their alphabet it can be damn hard. (I cannot even imagine)

![picture alt](/img/another-completely-new/languages.gif)

People having Latin letters in their native alphabet just skip the burden of how letters are pronounced but then comes the problem of sentence formation and exceptional cases. There languages which Google Translate can help up to a point. (for certain languages)

After practicing English for 30-something years, I now set sail to  learn Dutch. Previously in my twenties, I wanted to learn German and put serious effort to get to a point. My motivation eventually eroded and I was again at square #1.  Then I wanted to learn French - not aware that I was pushing the bar higher tough and nobody warned me 😊  

![picture alt](/img/another-completely-new/dutch.gif)
  
I tried Michel Thomas method via his courses. They were at first seem to be working but then I could not hold on to learning constantly, life happenned and again hello square #1.

One thing many people could be sure of is that you have to expose yourself to the language as much as possible.
...and that is not possible if you live in the Netherlands or Nordic countries. People can speak native-level English and don't bother to switch to English just in case you don't understand what they say.  

![picture alt](/img/another-completely-new/english.gif)
 
    
I wish that I was trapped in an island with people who knows only Dutch and no other language 😊
It could be utopic but would create a super-duper environment to learn this language.
